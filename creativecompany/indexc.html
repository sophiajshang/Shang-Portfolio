The internet is our most open global resource.
Thanks to the development of the Internet.

July 1961

Leonard Kleinrock at MIT published the first paper on packet switching theory. Kleinrock convinced Roberts of the theoretical feasibility of communications using packets rather than circuits. This is the first major step toward computer networking.
August 1962

The first recorded description of the social interactions enabled through networking was a in a series of memos discussing potential “Galactic Network” written by J.C.R. Licklider of MIT.
1965

MIT researcher Lawrence G. Roberts connected the TX-2 computer in Mass. to the Q-32 in California with a low speed dial-up telephone line creating the first (however small) wide-area computer network ever built.
Late 1966

Roberts working at DARPA develop the computer network concept and quickly put together his plan for the “ARPANET”.
